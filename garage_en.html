Fenice

Fenice, the second prototype built, was designed during the pandemic and built despite the problems of lack of microprocessor availability. It possesses a telemetry solution inspired by Formula One cars.

At the Riccardo Paletti racetrack in Varano de' Melegari, the team passed the three static tests, competing against 30 other international teams in the electric-powered category: the judges evaluated the prototype by awarding it third place for the engineering design test, 15th place for the business presentation test and 19th place for the cost report test.

The team also participated in a number of dynamic tests, obtaining tenth place in the autocross (timed course of about 800 m to be completed as quickly as possible) and completing the endurance (22-km test aimed at evaluating the reliability of the vehicle) in sixth place.

Chimera

Chimera was E-Agle Trento Racing Team's first creation, designed and built specifically for Formula Student competition. The car features an innovative performance design that incorporates advanced technologies to maximize performance, efficiency and reliability.

Chimera has a carbon-fiber body that makes it sleek, aerodynamic, lightweight and sturdy. Two rear electric motors and an advanced battery pack ensure high acceleration, which is critical in this type of competition. The car is equipped with optimized suspension and braking systems, which enable precise control of the vehicle on the track.

The team also incorporated advanced data acquisition and telemetry systems into the car, which allow real-time monitoring of the vehicle's status and provides useful data for deciding what modifications to make. Chimera was designed and built entirely by team members, who invested countless hours of research, design, testing and fabrication to create a car that represents the cutting edge of electric race car technology.

Overall, Chimera was a remarkable achievement and a testament to the skills, knowledge and dedication of the E-Agle Trento Racing team. Proving that the University of Trento could compete with other universities and laying the foundation for the team's future. Though the vehicle was carefully designed, it never passed inspection. But by learning from mistakes and improving over time, the design quality increased significantly, leading to better and better results.
